Jake Wood

My Life

As a native Oregonian, life began for me in the autumn of 1986 in NW Portland. Although I spent most of my preadult life exploring the high desert of Central Oregon, I made my return to Portland to pursue a degree from Portland State and begin life away from anything familiar. With that said, I felt at home in Portland.


Upon receiving my bachelor's degree in history, I moved away to Milwaukee, WI for a change of scenery and an attempt to start anew. I enjoyed experiencing the mid-western culture, sophisticating my beer palate, and the overexaggerated winters. Though I never took root in Milwaukee and relocated back to Portland after a mere two years, it was a memorable growing experience, which helped lead me toward looking into a career in social work/education.


Back in the PNW, I embraced Portland as my city and took advantage of everything I had neglected during my first stretch-exploring restaurants, seeing shows, attending Blazers and Timbers games, visiting every brewery in the area, hiking, camping, biking, etc. I joined AmeriCorps for a year and taught elementary-aged students to discover if I ultimately wanted to pursue a career in education or social work. My experiences with AmeriCorps were rewarding and a lot of fun, however I learned I was not as passionate about a lifelong career in that field.


I spent some time reevaluating a direction I wanted to take my life and found myself enrolled in the PDX Code Guild Bootcamp, where I gained valuable skills to develop a career in web development.
